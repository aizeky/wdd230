Cardiac Catheterization – Electrophysiology Study (EPS)

Electrophysiology studies (EPS) are tests that provide an understanding of the nature of abnormal heart rhythms (arrhythmias).

​Electrophysiology studies test the electrical activity of your heart to find where an arrhythmia (abnormal heartbeat) is coming from.

These studies take place in a special room called an electrophysiology (EP) lab or catheterization (cath) lab

During EPS, a thin tube called a catheter is inserted into a blood vessel that leads to your heart. A specialized electrode catheter designed for EP studies lets them send electrical signals to your heart and record its electrical activity.​​

    An EPS study can tell physicians:

  • Where the arrhythmia is coming from
  • How well certain medications are working to correct the arrhythmia
  • If a cardiac ablation is needed
